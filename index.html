About me

Hey there! I'm Younus Rahman, a Dot Net system developer. Born in Dhaka, Bangladesh, in 1987. I am a socially and structurally structured person. I enjoy socializing with people and going on trips. Panta bhat ( which is made of boiling rice that has been steeped and fermented in water) & fried elish(hilsa fish) is one of my favorite dishes. I also like to eat snacks like fochka (It consists of a hollow, crispy edible ball that is filled with mashed, cooked potatoes, chickpeas, and a variety of spices.)

I've always wanted to work in IT, but finding employment in this field has been challenging because I don't have any formal training in the field. I began working in healthcare instead of squandering time, but I kept looking for IT positions with straightforward tasks, such as networking approaches and training for the IT industry. I had my first opportunity to work as a network technician with Ericsson. Although I had a terrific time there, I quickly realized that coding was more my thing than anything else. When the Corona epidemic hit in 2019, I finally had the chance to pursue my higher education. In 2022, I became a system developer and started working at Karolinska University Hospital as a developer. Ever since, each and every day has felt like a dream to me. There are a ton of new things to discover, and the best part is that everyone around them has similar ambitions. What motivates me is the knowledge that everything we do is most beneficial to society. This amazing sensation is indescribable.

Overall, the most major changes in my life occurred in the period from 2018 to 2022. The world's greatest emotion and biggest duty came when I became a father. Bringing my family to Sweden was the second challenge. This procedure was meant to be simple and straightforward, but it turned out to be considerably more challenging because of a few minor mistakes. I was eventually given permission to take my family to Sweden in 2019.. I later that same year got my driver's license, which was pricey but necessary to keep everything together and conserve energy. After going through many courses and tests, I was able to enroll in Newton. Then Pandamie began, which caused everyone to become anxious and unhappy, yet during that time, God provided excellent news. Soon I will have another child. I was both excited and worried at the same time. I struggled to understand how I would handle it. I was taken aback by how my God guided me through everything and how easy it all was. I will always be grateful to all of the health and social care professionals in Sweden as well as to the Swedish government for giving us the freedom to pursue our dreams at any age.

Last but not least, my beautiful wife, I can't begin to tell you how grateful I am to have you in my life. My life has been blessed with so many good events since we first met. I feel like a new guy because of you! I appreciate that about you. You bring me back to vigor, joy, and hope. If you don't realize it, you are the one who motivates me. The light in this gloomy world is you. You are a person who has taught me, cared for me, and loved me at the same time. It is people like you who will make a difference. You have had an incredible influence on my life. More than any other words, I love you. The many beautiful events that have occurred in my life since this time have been experienced by us. You have my undying love for them. Until my last breath, I will love and respect you.

I regret not having a deeper understanding of several things in life.It took me 18 years to realize how important it is to have and follow certain rules in life. When the profession starts feeling dreadful every day, change the profession. The next question that pops up is: Which profession should I choose? Take a deep breath, reflect on your long-term objectives, and then move forward. Consider happiness, not bank account, Avoid searching for happiness in objects, goods, or products. Last but not least, avoid setting unnecessary targets. When unforeseen events occur in real life, fantasies can crumble into tiny pieces. Then, recovering is exceedingly difficult and, in some circumstances, impossible. I think a smaller target such as a week or a month long target will give less joy but more frequent rewards and the most crucial and nicest aspect of this enjoyment is that it inspires and increases self-confidence.
